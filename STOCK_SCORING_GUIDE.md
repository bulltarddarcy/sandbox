## üéØ THE GOAL: Catch the Wave Early, Don't Chase the Ship

**What This System Is Designed For:**
This scoring system is built to identify stocks **at the inflection point** - those transitioning from neutral/lagging into outperformance. We want to catch rotation INTO themes and stocks BEFORE they become obvious leaders.

**What This System Is NOT:**
This is not designed to highlight stocks that are already leading and extended. If a stock has alpha of +8% and has been outperforming for weeks, that's great if you're already in it - but it's **too late to enter**. This system will actually **penalize** those setups because the best risk/reward has passed.

**Trading Philosophy:**
- ‚úÖ **Catch the turn** ‚Üí Enter when momentum shifts from negative to positive
- ‚úÖ **Ride the middle** ‚Üí Capture the bulk of the move (days 1-15)
- ‚úÖ **Exit before the top** ‚Üí Take profits when extended
- ‚ùå **Don't chase** ‚Üí Avoid stocks already up 10-15% in their move

**Think of it like surfing:** You want to catch the wave as it's forming (paddling position), not after it's already cresting. By the time everyone sees it's a great wave, it's too late to paddle in.

## üìê The Scoring System (0-100 Points)

**Total: 100 Points Possible**

### Breakdown:
1. **Setup Quality (40 points)** - Is this stock at an inflection point?
2. **Entry Timing (30 points)** - Is RIGHT NOW the best time to enter?
3. **Risk/Reward (20 points)** - How much upside vs downside?
4. **Sector Context (10 points)** - Is the sector at the right stage?

**Letter Grades:**
- **A (80-100):** Elite setup - highest conviction entry
- **B (70-79):** Strong setup - good entry opportunity
- **C (60-69):** Decent setup - selective or small position
- **D (50-59):** Marginal - pass unless special situation
- **F (0-49):** Poor - avoid completely

## üéØ FACTOR 1: Setup Quality (40 Points)

**Purpose:** Identify stocks at the **beginning** of outperformance, not the end.

### Part A: Position Relative to Sector (20 points)

This measures where the stock is in its cycle relative to the sector. The best setups are when the sector is strong but the stock hasn't moved yet.

#### **Catching Up Play: +20 points** (BEST SETUP)

**Criteria (ALL must be true):**
1. Sector is Leading or Improving (score 70+)
2. Stock alpha 5-day: 0% to +2% (neutral to slightly positive)
3. Stock alpha 20-day: -2% to 0% (was lagging, now turning)
4. RVOL 5-day: 1.2x+ (volume confirming the turn)

**Why This Scores Highest:**
The sector is already hot (institutions are rotating IN), but this stock hasn't participated yet. This is the classic "last to wake up" play. You're catching it at the inflection point.

**Real Example:**
```
Technology Sector: 85 (A) - Early Stage Leadership (Day 3)

Stock: INTC
- Sector Alpha: The sector is outperforming by 3-5%
- Stock Alpha 20d: -1.5% (was underperforming)
- Stock Alpha 10d: -0.3% (turning neutral)
- Stock Alpha 5d: +0.8% (just turned positive!)
- RVOL: 1.4x (volume picking up)

Score: +20 points ‚úì

Translation: "Tech sector is hot. INTC was lagging but just started 
moving. Perfect timing to enter before it catches up to peers."
```

#### **Emerging Leader: +15 points** (CLEAN SETUP)

**Criteria (ALL must be true):**
1. Sector is Improving (building momentum)
2. Stock alpha 5-day: +1% to +3% (slightly leading)
3. Stock showing acceleration: 5d alpha > 10d alpha > 20d alpha
4. RVOL 5-day: 1.2x+

**Why This Scores High:**
Both the sector and stock are building momentum together. This is a clean, early-stage setup where everything is confirming at once.

## ‚è∞ FACTOR 2: Entry Timing (30 Points)

**Purpose:** Determine if RIGHT NOW is the optimal time to enter, or if you should wait.

### Part A: Price Position (15 points)

Where is the price relative to key technical levels? Best entries are near support or at fresh breakouts.

#### **Near Support (Pullback Entry): +15 points**

**Criteria (ALL must be true):**
1. Price within 2% of 21 EMA OR within 3% of 50 SMA
2. Price still above 200 SMA (long-term uptrend intact)
3. Recent low held (didn't break support)
4. Volume drying up on pullback (sign of no selling pressure)

**Why This Scores Highest:**
You're buying at a technical support level with a clear stop. Risk/reward is excellent here.

**Real Example:**
```
Stock: MSFT

Price: $425
21 EMA: $423 (within 0.5% - AT support)
50 SMA: $418
200 SMA: $390

Recent action: Pulled back to 21 EMA, bounced

Score: +15 points ‚úì

Translation: "Perfect entry at support. If it breaks $420, 
you're out. If it bounces, you're in early."
```

#### **Fresh Breakout: +15 points**

**Criteria (ANY trigger):**
1. Price crossed above 50 SMA within last 5 days
2. Price crossed above 21 EMA with volume spike
3. Price breaking out of consolidation range (3+ weeks)

**Why This Scores Highest:**
Breakouts are high-probability entries. You're catching the move as it starts.

**Real Example:**
```

Was consolidating $40-$42 for 4 weeks
Yesterday broke out to $42.50

Score: +15 points ‚úì

Translation: "Fresh breakout just happened. Enter now

```

#### **Tight Consolidation: +12 points**

**Criteria:**
1. ATR (volatility) has contracted by 30%+ vs 30-day average
2. Price range narrowing (Bollinger Bands squeezing)
3. Volume declining (coiling)

**Why This Scores High:**
Volatility contraction often precedes expansion. This is the calm before the storm.

**Real Example:**
```

Normal daily range: $5
Last 10 days range: $2 (ATR down 60%)
Bollinger Bands: Tightest in 2 months
```

#### **Neutral Zone: +8 points**

**Criteria:**
- Price between 21 EMA and 50 SMA
- No clear breakout or support test
- Normal volatility

**Why This Scores Moderate:**
Not a perfect entry, but not bad either. Neutral timing.

#### **Extended from Entry: -10 points** (PENALTY)

**Criteria (ANY trigger):**
1. Price >10% above 21 EMA
2. Price >15% above 50 SMA
3. No consolidation in 2+ weeks (parabolic move)

**Why This Gets Penalized:**
Price has moved too far too fast. Likely to consolidate or pull back. Wait.
